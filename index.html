Nguyen Anh Minh MAI
Computer Vision Research Engineer

I am (CIFRE) Ph.D. candidate at Paul Sabatier Toulouse III. I am also member of Toulouse Institute of Computer Science Research (IRIT) (coMputational Imaging anD viSion (MINDS) team), a joint research unit of French National Centre for Scientific Research (CNRS) and Cerema Center Research (Intelligent Transport Systems (ITS) team), where I work on computer vision and machine learning with a focus on scene understanding for autonomous vehicles. I supervised by Dr. Louahdi Khoudour, Dr. Pierre Duthon, Dr. Alain Crouzil, Prof. Dr. Sergio A. Velastin, Dr. Pascal Housam Salmane. We are in collaboration with Easymile.

In 2019, I got my Engineer's degree (a parallel "Master 2" degree) at National Institutes of Applied Sciences Centre-Val de Loire (INSA CVL) in Bourges, France. I went through internships at Mathematics, Image and Applications (MIA) lab (2018) supervised by Dr. Christophe Saint-Jean, and at French Alternative Energies and Atomic Energy Commission (CEA) (2019) supervised by Dr. Brice Burger, Dr. Mohamed Chaouch both on the research topic of 2D object detection. Besides, I was also Research Scientist at VinAI Research where I worked closely with Mau Dzung and Dr. Luu Khoa. Here, I worked on LiDAR-based 3D object detection.
